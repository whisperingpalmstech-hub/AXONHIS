2. Introduction
2.1 Purpose
This Functional Requirements Document (FRD) defines the functional and non-functional requirements for the Emergency Room (ER) module within the Hospital Information System (HIS). The ER module is designed to provide immediate emergency care management for patients with acute illnesses or injuries, ensuring rapid triage, treatment, and discharge processes.

2.3 Module Context within HIS
The Emergency Room module is a critical component of the HIS platform, serving as the primary entry point for emergency patients. It integrates seamlessly with multiple HIS modules including: 
Front Office - Patient registration and appointment management
Billing - Emergency service billing and payment collection
IPD (In-Patient Department) - Patient admission and transfer
OPD (Out-Patient Department) - Follow-up consultations
Nursing - Nursing assessments and care documentation
Doctor Desk - Clinical consultation and treatment orders
Laboratory (LIS) - Lab test orders and results
Radiology (RIS) - Imaging orders and reports
Pharmacy - Medication orders and dispensing
Inventory - Medical supplies and consumables
OTIMS - Emergency surgical procedures
EHR - Electronic health records integration

The ER module is built on the HIS SAAS (Software as a Service) platform developed by Axonic Health Pvt Ltd. It leverages cloud-based infrastructure for scalability, real-time data access, and seamless integration with other hospital systems. The architecture supports:
Real-time patient tracking and monitoring
Multi-user concurrent access with role-based permissions
Automated workflow management and alerts
Integration with medical devices and monitoring equipment
Cloud-hosted with local data storage options
API-based integration with external systems
Web-based access

5. Functional Requirements
5.1 ER Patient Registration
5.1.1 Urgent Registration
For unconscious or critical patients requiring immediate treatment:
System shall allow quick registration with minimal patient information
Accept temporary patient details (e.g., "Unknown Patient")
Generate temporary ER number instead of UHID
Allow approximate age entry
Support MLC case marking during registration
Capture accompanying person details
Allow conversion of ER number to UHID after patient identification
5.1.2 Normal Registration
For conscious patients with complete information:
System shall capture complete patient demographics (name, DOB, age, gender)
Capture contact information (mobile, email, address)
Capture identification details (ID type, ID number) 
Capture next of kin information
Support VIP patient marking
Capture race, occupation, religion, marital status, education
Capture nationality and language preferences 
Support permanent and local address entry
Generate unique UHID for new patients
Retrieve existing patient records for returning patients
Validate mandatory fields before saving 
5.2 Triage Management
5.2.1 Triage Assessment1. Manual Triage Assessment 2.Voice to Text functionality for triage assessment 
System shall display triage worklist for all registered ER patients
System shall support triage category selection:
  - Resuscitation (Red)
  - Emergent/Immediate (Red)
  - Urgent/Delayed (Yellow)
  - Less Urgent/Minimal (Light Green)
  - Non-Urgent (Light Green)
  - Dead/Expectant (Grey)
Use color coding for visual priority identification
Support allocation type selection (ER, Observation, Resuscitation)
Allow bed allocation based on triage category
Timestamp triage assessment
Track triage nurse details
Support triage reassessment
Generate alerts for high-priority patients
5.3 ER Patient Management
5.3.1 ER Patient List 
System shall display all active ER patients
Display of Medication details, Active medications & Investigation Details 
System shall show patient details, ward, bed number, triage category 
Display DOA and PDD
System shall provide action buttons for each patient
Support filtering by triage category, ward, date
Support patient search functionality
Show real-time patient status updates
5.3.2 Map of Bed
System shall display visual bed map of ER
System shall use color coding for bed status:
- Pink: Female patient occupied
        - Blue: Male patient occupied
        - Orange: Reserved bed
        - Green: Available bed
System shall display patient name, UHID, DOA, PDD on bed
Show bed number clearly
Provide quick access to nursing cover sheet from bed
Display patient diagnostics icons(lab and radiology services)
Show allergy icon, POC icon which should redirect to respective screen 
Support bed reservation
System shall allow bed transfer
System shall update bed status in real-time
5.4 Clinical Documentation

6. Process Workflows
6.1 ER Patient Journey Workflow
Complete patient flow through ER:
Patient Arrival → Registration (Urgent/Normal)
Registration → Triage Assessment
Triage → Bed Allocation
Bed Allocation → Clinical Assessment (Nursing Cover Sheet)
Clinical Assessment → Doctor Consultation
Doctor Consultation → Treatment Orders (Lab/Radiology/Pharmacy)
Treatment Orders → Service Execution
Service Execution → Monitoring and Documentation
Monitoring → Discharge Decision
Discharge Decision → Due for Discharge
Due for Discharge → Billing
Billing → Payment Settlement
Payment Settlement → Final Discharge
Final Discharge → Bed Vacated
6.2 MLC Case Workflow
Patient Arrival (MLC Case) → Urgent Registration
Mark as MLC during registration
Capture MLC details (Police station Name, FIR No, Mode of Arrival etc.)
Proceed with standard ER workflow
Complete MLC reporting worklist
Upload MLC documents
Generate MLC report (MIS Reports)
Complete treatment and discharge process
6.3 Triage Workflow
Patient registered → Appears in Triage Worklist
Triage nurse assesses patient
Assign triage category based on severity
Select allocation type (ER/Observation/Resuscitation)
Allocate appropriate bed
Save triage assessment
Patient moves to ER Patient List
High-priority patients generate alerts
6.4 Discharge Workflow
Doctor decides discharge → Initiate discharge request
Select discharge reason (Normal/DAMA/LAMA/Death/Absconding)
System validates pending services
Patient moves to "Marked for Discharge"
Billing executive generates final bill
Payment collection and settlement
Patient moves to "Ready for Billing"
Submit bill → Generate final receipt
Final discharge → Vacate bed
Patient removed from active ER list
Records archived in EHR

Notification and alerts for critical events
Workflow status tracking and monitoring
Dashboard updates for real-time status
