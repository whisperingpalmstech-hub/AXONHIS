2. Introduction
2.1 Purpose
This Functional Requirements Document (FRD) defines the functional and non-functional requirements for the In-Patient Department (IPD) Module of the Hospital Information System (HIS). The IPD module is designed to manage the complete lifecycle of inpatient care, from admission through discharge, including bed management, nursing care, clinical documentation, patient transfers, and billing integration.

2.3 Module Context within HIMS
The IPD Module is a core component of the HIS platform and serves as the central hub for inpatient care management. It integrates seamlessly with multiple HIS modules to provide comprehensive patient care:
Front Office: Patient registration, admission requests, and visitor management
Billing: IPD charges, final billing, deposit management, and payment settlement
Pharmacy: Medication orders, dispensing, and administration tracking
Laboratory (LIS): Lab test orders, sample collection, and result integration
Radiology (RIS): Imaging orders, scheduling, and report integration
Doctor Desk: Clinical documentation, diagnosis, treatment planning
Nursing Station: Nursing assessments, care plans, vital monitoring
OTIMS: Surgical admissions and OT scheduling integration
Inventory/Store: Material consumption and stock management
Back Office: Administrative processing and documentation
Finance: Revenue tracking and financial reporting
EHR: Electronic health records and patient history

3. System Architecture and Integration
3.1 Module Architecture
The IPD Module follows a multi-tier architecture integrated within the HIS platform:
Presentation Layer: Web-based user interface accessible via browsers
Application Layer: Business logic for admission, nursing, clinical workflows
Integration Layer: APIs for seamless communication with other HIS modules
Data Layer: Centralized HIS database with IPD-specific tables
Security Layer: HIS SSO and RBAC for access control

5. Functional Requirements
5.1 Admission Management
5.1.1 Admission Request
Description: Initiate admission request for a patient requiring inpatient care.
Functional Requirements:
System shall allow Front Office staff to create admission requests
 System shall capture patient demographics (name, gender, DOB, mobile, blood group)
Allow selection of admitting doctor specialty and treating doctor
FR-IPD-004: System shall capture admission category (emergency, planned, day care)
Allow entry of reason for admission
Capture date of admission and bed category preference
Generate unique admission request ID upon save
Route admission request to pending admission queue
5.1.2 Pending Admission Processing
Description: Review and process pending admission requests.
Functional Requirements:
Display all pending admission requests in a worklist
Allow Admission Desk staff to select and process requests
Display patient details, doctor details, and bed requirements
Show real-time bed availability status
Allow approval or rejection of admission requests
Shall generate confirmation message upon processing
5.1.3 Bed Reservation and Allocation
Description: Reserve and allocate beds for admitted patients.
Functional Requirements:
Allow bed reservation before admission
Shall display available beds by category, ward, and floor
System shall allow selection of bed based on patient category
System shall support bed allocation for different billing categories
Capture payment entitlement (self, insurance, corporate)
System shall allow entry of planned discharge date (PDD)
Reserve bed and update bed status to "Occupied"
Prevent double booking of beds
5.1.4 Bill Estimation
Description: Provide cost estimation for inpatient stay.
Functional Requirements:
System shall allow creation of bill estimation at admission
Allow addition of services (bed charges, procedures, tests)
Calculate total estimated cost based on services
System shall allow application of discounts as per hospital policy
Generate printable bill estimation document
System shall display service-wise cost breakdown
5.1.5 Patient Registration and Admission
Description: Complete patient registration and admission process.
Functional Requirements:
System shall convert admission request to full registration
Capture identification details (type, number)
Capture race, occupation, and other demographics
Allow entry of patient meal preferences
Shall allow entry of attendant meal preferences
Capture patient class and category
Allow entry of admission remarks
Capture treating doctor details
Allow entry of referral details (type, source)
Support next of kin information capture
Allow deposit collection at admission
Support provisional and final diagnosis entry (ICD-10)
Display order sets mapped to diagnosis
Allow visitor pass generation
Allow reference number creation
Support MLC case marking with priority
System shall capture MLC details (police station, FIR, buckle number)
System shall generate unique UHID if patient is new
System shall generate admission number upon completion
System shall generate "Registration Cum Admission" form

Support bill-wise and group-wise discounts
System shall support percentage and amount-wise discounts
Calculate doctor share
Generate final bill upon save

6. Process Workflows
6.1 Complete IPD Admission Workflow
1. Front Office creates admission request
2. Admission Desk processes pending admission
3. Admission Desk allocates bed
4. Admission Desk completes patient registration
5. System generates admission number and UHID
6. Nursing staff accepts admission in workstation
7. Nursing staff creates nursing coversheet
8. Nursing staff documents complaints, history, allergies
9. Nursing staff records vitals and initial assessment
10. Nursing staff adds orders as per doctor advice
11. Doctor logs in and accesses IPD patient
12. Doctor reviews nursing documentation
13. Doctor adds diagnosis and treatment plan
14. Doctor adds clinical orders
15. Orders routed to respective departments (Lab, Radiology, Pharmacy)
16. Services executed and results updated
17. Ongoing nursing care and monitoring
18. Progress notes updated continuously
6.2 Patient Transfer Workflow
1. Nursing staff initiates bed transfer
2. System validates destination bed availability
3. Nursing staff enters transfer reason and notes
4. System generates transfer slip
5. System updates bed status (vacate source, occupy destination)
6. Transfer request routed to destination nursing station
7. Destination nursing staff reviews transfer request
8. Destination nursing staff accepts or rejects transfer
9. System updates patient location
10. System generates updated transfer slip
11. Patient physically moved to new location
12. Destination nursing staff continues care
6.3 Discharge Workflow
1. Doctor determines patient is ready for discharge
2. Nursing staff initiates discharge request
3. System validates all services are rendered
4. Nursing staff submits ready for billing
5. Billing staff generates final bill
6. System calculates all charges (services, medications, bed, procedures)
7. System applies discounts and uses deposits
8. Billing staff presents bill to patient/family
9. Patient makes payment (full or partial)
10. Billing staff settles bill in system
11. System generates final bill receipt
12. Nursing staff performs final discharge
13. Nursing staff vacates bed
14. System updates bed status to "Available"
15. System generates discharge summary
16. Patient leaves hospital with discharge documents
